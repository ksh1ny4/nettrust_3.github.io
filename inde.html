    WHAT IS PASSWORD

    A password is a combination of characters used to authenticate and protect access to personal accounts, devices, or online services. Its main objective is to guarantee the privacy and security of our personal information. By creating a password, we are establishing a barrier of protection for our accounts and data.

    WHAT ARE COOKIES USED FOR?

    A strong password should be complex and unique enough to avoid being guessed or cracked by hackers or malware. Here are some key features we should consider when creating a strong password: 1. Appropriate length: It is recommended that it have a minimum length of 8 characters. The longer the password, the more difficult it will be to crack. 2. Combination of characters: Must contain a combination of letters (upper and lower case), numbers and special characters (such as !, @, #, $, %). 3. Avoid obvious personal information: Avoid using obvious personal information, such as your name, date of birth or phone numbers. Hackers can easily obtain this information and use it to guess your password. 4. Avoid predictable sequences or patterns: Avoid using predictable numerical sequences or patterns, such as "123456" or "abcdef." These passwords are easy to guess and should be avoided.

    HOW DO COOKIES WORK?

    It is crucial to be careful when creating a password because this ensures the security of our personal information and prevents possible intrusions into our privacy. A weak password can be easily cracked, putting our bank accounts, sensitive data, and other important aspects of our digital lives at risk.
